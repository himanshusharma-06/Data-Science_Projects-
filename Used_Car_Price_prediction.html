Data Analysis using Python

Problem Statement: Consider a case in which we want to sell our used car for a perfect price i.e, neither too high and nor too low. For this scenario we have to take various features of the car like performance, condition, color etc. into consideration and then we can set a good price for the car. This can be achieved using data analysis on the old cars dataset containing various features of the car as the attributes. The dataset of old cars is available on the link: https://archive.ics.uci.edu/ml/machine-learning-databases/autos/imports-85.data

ANOVA(Analysis of Variance): It is a method for statistical comparison of groups. It is used to find correlation between different groups of a categorical variable.It returns following values:

  1. F-test score: variation between sample group mean divided by variatio within sample group.
  2. p-value: confidence degree
Large F-value and small p-value denote strong correlation.

Conclusion:

  • If the F-value/F-statistics is less than 1 and p-value is greater than 0.05 then, we can say that the two data groups are not significantly different.
    Example: anova_result_1 => statistics = 0.197(less than 1) and p-value = 0.66(greater than 0.05) therefore the two groups are not signiicantly different.
  • If the F-value/F-statistics is greater than 1 and p-value is less than F value or 0.05 then, we can say that the two data groups are significantly different.
    Example: anova_result_2

Correlation: It is the statistical metric for measuring the level of independency between variables.

  • Measuring strength of correlation:
    1. Correlation coefficient:
      • close to +1: large +ve correlation
      • close to -1: large -ve correlation
      • close to 0: No relation
    2. p-value:
      • p-value < 0.001 -> strong certainity in relation
      • p-value < 0.05 -> moderate
      • p-value < 0.1 -> weak
      • p-value > 0.1->No certainity
